DESIGN

Our main goal is to develop a device that, using Whole Cell Biosensors, implements a discarding system to find metabolites of interest. At the same time, we want to apply the discoverings found on these with an actual real life illness Since our main goal is to reduce the possibility of bacterial infections on CF patients, we developed a two step plan work which consists of a mucolytic treatment and whole-cell biosensors that facilitate the discovery of new antibiotics.

Thus we established two objectives:

  1. To produce a recombinant protein (sialidase) able to reduce mucus viscosity in CF patients using a sfGFP that helps secrete the sialidase out of the cell, facilitating the recovery process.
  2. To produce 5 whole cell biosensors able to detect the mechanism of action of unknown compounds with possible antibiotic properties.

Whole cell biosensors designs

Each biosensor produces a different fluorescent signal depending on the mechanism of action that the substance is affecting, interrupting the biosynthesis of essential molecules for the cell, such as DNA, RNA, etc.

First, we looked for the main promoters that control transcription when B. subtilis is under stress due to antibiotic exposure, each of them induces a different type of self defense mechanism, the chosen promoters are listed:

Promoter Mechanism of action
helD Protein Synthesis
bmrC Fatty acid biosynthesis
yorB DNA biosynthesis
fabHB RNA biosynthesis
ypuA Cell wall synthesis

Once these biosensors are created, they will be used during field research when researchers suspect of the presence of a new antibiotic. However, further analysis will be required to determine if the antibiotics are indeed new or not. To address this, our team has developed a microfluidic device that will encapsulate actinomycetes (the most antibiotic-producing bacteria) by droplets with the biosensor.

Once a fluorescent signal is produced, the droplet will be destroyed with electrospray by a mass spectrophotometer to detect all the signals of the present compounds. A comparison with a library of the signals of the already known compounds will be made, and if a peak in the measurement does not match, then it could be a potential new antibiotic. Considering that the production of the GFP by B. subtilis may be too low due to the stress the bacteria is being subjected to, we will also use an amplification cascade to magnify the signal of the fluorescent protein.
