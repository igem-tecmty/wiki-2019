RESULTS

Goals

At the beginning of Breathe-in, we decided to set modular objectives in order to achieve our main goal. The modules were not divided into the typical iGem areas but according to the intentions of the different areas that compose the design section. We also included the mathematical model to have a proper complementarity between the theoretical and experimental work.

Whole cell biosensors

  • To assemble five different constructs that will allow us to detect the presence of molecules generating different types of cellular damage.
  • To assemble constructs that allow us to amplify the signal and reduce the limit of detection of the different biosensors.
  • To characterize both the amplified and non amplified biosensors.

Microfluidic platform

  • To create a microfluidic device that could multiflex the amount of reactions for the detection of antibiotics.
  • To produce Bacillus subtilis culture droplets.
  • To simulate the conditions of biosensors activation in droplets with the use of antibiotics.

Mucolytic

  • To characterize an easy method for protein recovery for further assays.
  • To measure the effect of sialidase employment over the viscosity of a CF lung simulated environment.
  • To increment the effect of antibiotics over P. aeruginosa by the addition of sialidases to the media.

Microfluidic platform

  • Designed and successfully manufactured microfluidic devices for droplet generation using soft photolithography techniques.
  • Modelled and simulated microfluidic geometries to validate the proposed flow rates in a T-junction using COMSOL Multiphysics.
  • Generated microdroplets in the microfluidic d evice with the validated conditions from the simulations.

Whole cells biosensors

3A assembly

  • The plasmid for B. subtilis genome integration (PBS1C) was successfully transformed and digested for the 3A assembly strategy, using EcoR1 and Pst1 restriction enzymes.
  • After amplification, the synthetized gblocks corresponding for each of the promoters were successfully ligated to an RGT part, using the pSB1C3 plasmid as a vector, digested and confirmed by running an electrophoresis gel.

Amplification cascade

The 5 promoters were successfully ligated to the cascade through the Golden Gate assembly method.

Mucolytic

  • E. Coli containing the corresponding constructs of this module were successfully transformed.
  • Both the bacteria transformed with the sfGFP and that with the chimeric protein showed to be highly expressed according to their phenotypes.
  • The expression of the chimeric protein was IPT induced.
  • The expressed chimeric protein was correctly purificated using the HisTag.
