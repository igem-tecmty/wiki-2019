HUMAN PRACTICES

Asociación Regiomontana de Fibrosis Quística

This association is dedicated to improve the life quality of patients with cystic fibrosis, in the state of Nuevo León, Mexico. They primarily give treatments and medications to patients with low incomes, and to achieve this, they organize fundraising events.

Through this contact we were able to meet members of the association, including children affected by this disease, which helped us to see the reality that patients live.

Meeting with Dr. Albino Rivas

Held in the campus, this meeting helped us to modify the focus of the project, specifically because he pointed out that in our country the treatment for CF patients is inaccessible for most of the population, due to its high cost, which reduces the life expectancy in Mexico. Originally, one of the objectives of the new mucolytic was to reduce the secondary effects of the commonly used treatments by encapsulating the drug and regulating its distribution in the body ().

Camp

We organized multiple activities with the children of the association, including games and experiments, based on e-Bug, an international platform that aims to reduce antibiotic resistance by creating activities that can make children comprehend the magnitude of this problematic.

Instituto Nacional de Salud Pública

In order to learn more about antibiotic resistance in our country, we contacted the National Institute of Public Health of Mexico. Thanks to the institute, we were able to talk with the members of the research line in medicines in public health about the access and usage of antibiotics and antimicrobial resistance.

Anahí Dreser

On May 16, a call was made with Anahi Dreser, a researcher specialized in antibiotic usage, health policies and quality of care of the National Institute of Public Health. With this call we were able to know a little more about the current situation of antibiotic resistance in Mexico and analyze alternatives to generate an impact on society. She told us that the magnitude of the problem itself is unknown. This can be associated to the lack of license in the collection of information by governmental entities.

In addition, there is a record that Mexican doctors tend to misuse antibiotics and that patients do not follow their prescribed treatment.

We could also discuss the impact that a device like the one we propose and the human practices we plan will have. Dr. Dreser said "It would be good that just as the new generations are growing with a culture of recycling, they could grow with a culture of the correct use of antibiotics".

Thanks to the information provided, we began to investigate the legislative situation of this issue, analyze the most resistant bacteria in our country and implement E-bug dynamics with patients more vulnerable to infections and with children of various ages.
